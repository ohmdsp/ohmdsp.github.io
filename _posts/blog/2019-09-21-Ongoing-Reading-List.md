With so many books, blogs, and articles to choose from, it can be helpful to get recommendations from others about what to read. This is my list of suggestions based on the books, blogs and articles that I have read that I feel are most enlightening and enjoyable. Note, that I will keep updating this list as I discover more treasures.
<br><br>
__Non-Fiction__:
<br>
 * __Seven Brief Lessons on Physics__, by Carlo Rovelli<br>
 This is a well-written book that is enjoyable to read and educational. 
 * __Reality Is Not What It Seems: The Journey to Quantum Gravity__, by Carlo Rovelli<br>
 I really enjoyed the background material presented in this book. It felt like a fresh approach to teaching, and the historical connections were enlightening. The later sections on quantum loop gravity were interesting, but less informative than the rest of the book.
 * __The Signal And The Noise__, by Nate Silver<br>
 This book is packed with information and stories related to statistical analysis of world events, weather patterns, natural disasters, markets, etc. I found it to be a little too wordy throughout and had to skim some sections in order to stay interested. However, it is worth reading if you want to hear about statistical prediction applied to many different modern history events. If you want to learn about the actual math behind statistical prediction, you will need to get a different book.
