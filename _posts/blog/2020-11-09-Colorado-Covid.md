The Covid pandemic has certainly captured the focus of 2020. Earlier this year we all made adjustments to almost every aspect of our lives. I cannot recall the last time I attended church, sat down to a meal at a resturant, or worked an entire day at the office without wearing a mask. I'm not saying the difficulty compares to other more severe challenges people have faced throughout history, but it has been different. <br>

We are told another wave of the pandemic is in progress, and the data suggests the number of positive cases has increased dramatically (see banner for Nov. 9th, 2020). We also just learned that Pfizer Corp is having success with a vaccine phase 3 trial. Since nothing like this has happened before, there is no solid science to base decisions and judgements on. After all, science requires emperical data from past experiments that match the current situation, and that is just not available. So, science is in the making along with many opportunities for policy decisions. What will Colorado decide to do in the weeks ahead. I will be curious to learn.
