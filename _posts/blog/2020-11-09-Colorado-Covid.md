The Covid pandemic has certainly captured the focus of 2020. Earlier this year we all made adjustments to almost every aspect of our lives. I cannot recall the last time I attended church, sat down to a meal at a resturant, or worked an entire day at the office without wearing a mask. I would not say the difficulty compares to other more severe difficulties people have faced thorughout history, but it has been different. <br>

We are told we are in another wave of the pandemic, and the data suggests the number of positive cases has risen dramatically (see banner from Nov. 9th, 2020). Since nothing like this has happened before, there is no solid science to base decisions and judgements on. After all, science requires emperical data from past experiments that match the current situation and that is just not available. So, science is in the making along with many opportunities for policy decisions. What will Colorado decide to do in the weeks ahead. I will be curious to learn.
